<p><p>Optical tweezers and magnetic tweezers are advanced tools used in mechanobiology to manipulate micrometer-sized particles and biological samples. Optical tweezers utilize focused laser beams to trap and move particles, enabling detailed studies of molecular interactions and cellular processes. In contrast, magnetic tweezers apply magnetic fields to control magnetic beads attached to biomolecules, allowing for the investigation of mechanical properties and forces at the molecular level. Both technologies are essential in understanding biological mechanisms and dynamics.</p></p>
