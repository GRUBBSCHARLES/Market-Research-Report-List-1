<p><ul><li>Pre-Combustion Carbon Capture</li><li>Oxy-Combustion Carbon Capture</li><li>Post-Combustion Carbon Capture</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) is a technology aimed at reducing carbon dioxide emissions by capturing CO2 produced from industrial processes and storing it underground. </p><p>There are three main market types: </p><p>1. **Pre-Combustion Carbon Capture** involves capturing CO2 before combustion occurs, typically in gasification processes.</p><p>2. **Oxy-Combustion Carbon Capture** uses pure oxygen for combustion, producing a flue gas that is easier to capture.</p><p>3. **Post-Combustion Carbon Capture** captures CO2 from exhaust gases after fuel combustion has occurred, used widely in existing power plants and industrial facilities.</p></p>
