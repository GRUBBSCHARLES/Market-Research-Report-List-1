<p><p>The micro- and nano-encapsulation market involves the techniques of enclosing substances in microscopic or nanoscopic shells to enhance their properties and sustain their release. Microencapsulation typically refers to particles ranging from 1 to 1000 micrometers, often used in food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics to improve stability and controlled release. In contrast, nanoencapsulation deals with particles smaller than 100 nanometers, offering advantages like increased bioavailability and targeted delivery in drug formulations. Both methods are essential in various industries for improving product performance.</p></p>
