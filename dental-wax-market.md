<p><p>The dental wax market comprises various types, each serving specific purposes. Pattern wax is used for creating detailed dental patterns and models. Processing wax aids in the fabrication of prosthetics and dental restorations, ensuring precision during procedures. Impression wax captures accurate dental impressions for diagnostics and treatment planning. Healing wax, often employed post-surgery, provides protection to sensitive areas and fosters recovery. Together, these wax types are essential in dental practice, enhancing both functionality and patient care.</p></p>
