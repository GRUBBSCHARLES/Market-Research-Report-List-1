<p><p>The dry cell battery market comprises two main types: primary cells and secondary cells. Primary cells are non-rechargeable batteries, designed for single-use applications, often found in household items like remote controls and flashlights. In contrast, secondary cells are rechargeable batteries, allowing for multiple cycles of charging and discharging, making them ideal for consumer electronics, electric vehicles, and renewable energy storage. Both types serve distinct needs in various sectors, driving innovation and market growth.</p></p>
