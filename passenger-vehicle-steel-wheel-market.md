<p><p>The passenger vehicle steel wheel market comprises three primary types: casting, forging, and others. </p><p>Casting involves pouring molten steel into molds to create wheels, allowing for intricate designs but may reduce strength. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping heated steel under pressure, resulting in stronger and more durable wheels. The "other" category encompasses alternative manufacturing methods such as rolled or welded wheels, which cater to specific vehicle requirements or cost considerations. Each method offers distinct advantages in performance and manufacturing efficiency.</p></p>
