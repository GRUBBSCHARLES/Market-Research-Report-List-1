<p><p>The surrogacy service market comprises two primary types: gestational surrogacy and traditional surrogacy. Gestational surrogacy involves the surrogate carrying an embryo created through in vitro fertilization (IVF), where the surrogate has no genetic connection to the child. In contrast, traditional surrogacy entails the surrogate using her own egg, meaning she is both the genetic and gestational mother. The choice between these types depends on personal, medical, and legal considerations, shaping the overall dynamics of the surrogacy market.</p></p>
